Refund Policy

Corezality PTY Ltd (trading as Grdbase) · Last updated: May 2026

We build custom web systems. Because work begins immediately after deposit payment, our refund terms reflect the nature of bespoke development work.

1. The deposit

The 50% deposit is non-refundable once work has commenced. By paying the deposit you confirm you have read and agreed to these terms.

Work commences when we send your content kit and begin configuring your project. This typically happens within 1 business day of deposit payment.

The deposit covers the time spent on project setup, design configuration, system customisation, and initial build work — none of which can be recovered once started.

2. Refund scenarios

Situation Refund
You cancel before work commences (within 24 hours of deposit) Full deposit refund
You cancel after work has commenced No refund on deposit
We are unable to deliver the agreed scope Full refund of all amounts paid
You are unhappy with the preview after 2 revision rounds No refund — revisions are included to address concerns
Third-party service outage prevents delivery Timeline extended — no refund
You fail to provide content within agreed timeframe No refund — timeline shifts, project remains open
Monthly retainer — cancel with 30 days notice Current month non-refundable, future months cancelled

3. Balance payment

The remaining 50% balance is due before go-live. This payment is also non-refundable once the site has been made live and the domain pointed.

4. How to request a refund

If you believe you are entitled to a refund under these terms, contact us in writing at hello@grdbase.co.za within 7 days of the triggering event. Include your payment reference number and a description of the issue.

We will respond within 3 business days. Approved refunds are processed via PayFast and typically reflect within 5–7 business days depending on your bank.

5. Disputes

If you are unhappy with any aspect of our service, please contact us at hello@grdbase.co.za before initiating a chargeback or dispute with your bank or PayFast. We are committed to resolving issues fairly and directly.

Chargebacks initiated without prior communication will be contested. Fraudulent chargebacks may be reported to the relevant authorities.

6. Consumer Protection Act

Nothing in this policy limits your rights under the Consumer Protection Act, 68 of 2008. If you believe your statutory rights have been affected, you may contact the National Consumer Commission at thencc.gov.za.

7. Contact

For any questions about this policy: hello@grdbase.co.za
